### H2: What financed emissions are
Intent: Define financed emissions in plain terms and place them in the GHG Protocol accounting structure. 
Content:
- Financed emissions are the greenhouse gas emissions associated with a financial institution's loans and investments (established term from `methodology-alignment`).
- The core accounting concept is Scope 3 Category 15 (investments): when an institution holds equity or debt in a company, a share of that company's emissions is attributed to the institution based on proportional ownership (GHG Protocol- find appropriate source to link).
- Attribution is proportional, not a formula on this page: described in words as the institution's share of an investee. (Key claim, attribution-factor, rendered as prose, no formula per SME.)
- PCAF (Partnership for Carbon Accounting Financials) maintains the most widely used method, and its Standard is built on and recognized by the GHG Protocol (generally-accepted; framed first-party).
- When an institutional investor sets a net-zero target, it means the portfolio's attributable financed emissions are reduced and balanced to net zero by a target date, typically 2050 or earlier (SME philosophy).

### H2: Financed avoided emissions and forward-looking metrics
Intent: Introduce the two metric categories PCAF's supplement adds, with PCAF's own guardrails for optional, separate reporting. Link to the supplement: https://carbonaccountingfinancials.com/files/standard-launch-2025/PartA-PCAF-Supplement-Avoided-Emissions-FLM.pdf
Content:
- PCAF addresses these in a supplemental guidance document that accompanies the PCAF Standard: Part A, launched December 2, 2025, currently applicable to financed emissions across asset classes (verified from the supplement; facilitated and insurance-associated emissions may follow in future work).
- The supplement covers two distinct categories:
  - Financed avoided emissions: avoided emissions attributed to a financial institution that provides capital supporting a project, or a company avoiding emissions. The GHG Protocol defines avoided emissions as "the positive GHG emissions impact of a product (good or service), relative to the situation where that product does not exist." The supplement expands prior Part A guidance, which was previously limited to renewable power projects, to all asset classes, and connects to PCAF's Use of Proceeds (UoP) accounting guidance (verified from the supplement).
  - Forward-looking emissions metrics (FLM): emerging tools for the future decarbonization potential of a financial exposure, specifically Expected Emissions Reductions (EER), proposed by the GFANZ Secretariat, and Expected Avoided Emissions (EAE) (verified from the supplement). This is the same as avoided emissions (link to the appropriate internal avoided emissions terms page, sometimes EER is used instead of "avoided emissions")
- The GHG Protocol Corporate Standard does not yet provide a standard on avoided emissions but has published a neutral framework for estimating and disclosing positive GHG impacts; WBCSD published corporate guidance (verified from the supplement; ties to Koi's WBCSD / Project Frame canon).
- The GHG Protcol is developing a draft standard for "Action and Market Instruments" (AMI) with the standard expected in 2027. Consider information from this page and how it relates, basically they are going to be including mitigation strategies beyond direct operations, such as avoided emissions and climate solution portfolio composition, so the standards are evolving and Koi is ahead of the game. https://ghgprotocol.org/blog/ghg-protocol-launches-action-and-market-instruments-request-information-what-it-and-why-it (maybe this should be its own section? you decide)
- Reporting both categories is currently optional, given their early-stage nature and challenges with data availability, aggregation, and potential double counting. If an institution reports them, PCAF says they "shall report them separately from the regular financed emissions inventory" (verified wording from the supplement).


### H2: Why the two stay in separate ledgers
Intent: The credibility heart of the page. Explain why avoided emissions cannot be netted against financed emissions.
Content:
- Different ledgers, different questions. Financed emissions answer "what share of actual, present-day emissions is my capital associated with?" Avoided emissions answer "compared to a counterfactual baseline, how much lower are emissions because this solution exists or scaled?" The first is an attributional inventory; the second is a consequential, scenario-based estimate (SME philosophy).
- Why the separation matters: if a portfolio could offset its attributed Scope 1-3 with the avoided emissions of its EV makers, heat-pump manufacturers, and renewables developers, it could claim "net negative" while still financing substantial real-economy emissions. The counterfactual is hypothetical; the attributed emissions are relatively observed. Mixing them collapses the integrity of both (SME philosophy, EV example).
- This is not only Koi's view. PCAF's own supplement is explicit that both categories are "fundamentally distinct from financed (generated) emissions," that they "should not be aggregated for portfolio-level comparisons," and that they "shall not be used to substantiate net-zero commitments or claims," with caution advised to avoid misrepresenting the actual climate impact of loans or investments (verified verbatim from the supplement). The GHG Protocol's avoided-emissions framework and WBCSD guidance keep the two apart on the same logic (ties to WBCSD / Project Frame canon).

### H2: How avoided emissions fit a net-zero strategy
Intent: Show the practical reconciliation: parallel targets, not a single netted number.
Content:
- Credible commitments run two parallel targets: reduce financed emissions on an absolute basis against a 1.5C pathway (no avoided-emissions netting), and increase capital exposure to climate solutions, with avoided emissions as one impact metric for the latter (SME philosophy). Consistent with PCAF, avoided emissions and forward-looking metrics inform the climate-solutions story but do not substantiate the net-zero claim itself.
- Real-economy vs paper decarbonization: portfolio emissions can drop simply by selling high-emitting holdings to another investor, which changes nothing in the world. Credible strategies emphasize engagement and capital allocation toward solutions, with divestment as a signal or last resort (SME philosophy).
- Residual emissions at the target date are neutralized with durable carbon removals, not avoided-emissions offsets (SME philosophy; links to CDR classification).
- The discipline is refusing to collapse the two into one number, because the moment you do, the metric stops measuring anything coherent (SME philosophy, closing line).
- Where Koi fits: Koi measures the avoided-emissions / climate-solutions side with auditable, baseline-transparent models (link to Avoided Emissions, Classification, methodology alignment).
